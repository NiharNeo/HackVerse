The Current Highlight

As you stand before the mirrored surface of the Kyōko-chi (Mirror Pond), the audio guide delves into the architectural marvel of the top two floors, entirely covered in pure gold leaf.

Originally built as a retirement villa for the shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu in the late 14th century, the structure was converted into a Zen temple by his son, according to his wishes. The pavilion's design brilliantly harmonizes three distinct architectural styles: Shinden, Samurai, and Zen, reflecting the turbulent yet culturally rich Muromachi period.

Notice the bronze phoenix ornament standing proud on the roof. It survived the tragic arson of 1950, a story deeply explored in Chapter 3 of this series. The surrounding strolling garden, designed to integrate seamlessly with the natural landscape, represents the Buddhist concept of the Pure Land.

About the Narrator

Kenji Sato

Kenji Sato is a renowned historian and Kyoto native, specializing in the Muromachi period. With over two decades of experience as a lead curator at the Kyoto National Museum, his voice brings unparalleled depth and authenticity to the stories hidden within these ancient walls.
